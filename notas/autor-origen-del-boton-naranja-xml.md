<p class="intro">Vía <a href="http://simplebits.com/quickbits/">QuickBits</a> del renovado <a href="http://simplebits.com">SimpleBits</a>. Uno de los símbolos de la blogosfera: el <a href="http://www.scripting.com/images/xml.gif">botón naranja XML</a>.</p>
El botón naranja de RSS lo diseñó <a href="http://www.bryanbell.com/">Brian Bell</a> en diciembre de <a href="http://archive.scripting.com/2000/12/14#linkingToXml">2000</a>. Es especialista en iconos y creador de <a href="http://www.bryanbell.com/2002/07/10#a55">populares</a> <a href="http://www.bryanbell.com/myThemes">plantillas</a>:

Hace unos días comentaba el <a href="http://www.bryanbell.com/2004/12/14#a1249">origen</a>:
<blockquote><a href="http://www.bryanbell.com/2004/12/14#a1249">So that's where the orange XML icon came from</a> (14/12/2004)

Even though I made the little icon, I've since forgotten how it came to be. Thanks to Dave Winer's Weblog/time-machine the exact conversation has been preserved.

(...) In retrospect, if I knew it was going to be so popular I might have put a little more sex appeal into it, but at the time I was just doing a favor for a friend who asked for an orange block with XML written on it.</blockquote>
