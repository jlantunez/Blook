<p>Vía <a href="http://www.wired.com/news/mac/0,2125,66460,00.html">Wired News</a>. Perseguidos.</p>

<p>El 80% de los trabajadores de Microsoft en <acronym title="16.000 entre 25.000 personas">Redmond</acronym> que tienen un reproductor MP3 portátil tienen un <a href="http://apple.com/ipod">iPod</a>.</p>

<p>Según un alto cargo que quiere mantenerse en el anonimato:</p>

<blockquote>"These guys are really quite scared," said the source of Microsoft's management. "It shows how their backs are against the wall.... Even though it's Microsoft, no one is interested in what we have to offer, even our own employees."

So concerned is management, owning an iPod at Microsoft is beginning to become impolitic, the manager said. Employees are hiding their iPods by swapping the telltale white headphones for a less conspicuous pair.

"Some people are a bit concerned about being traitors, not supporting the company," he said. "They're a bit stealth about it."</blockquote>
<a href="http://www.technologymarketing.com/mc/content/article_display.jsp?vnu_content_id=1970587">Mary Jo Foley</a>, editora de <a href="http://www.microsoft-watch.com/">Microsoft Watch</a>, quizá el único periodista con <acronym title="a menudo acusada de pertenecer a la maquinaria de RRPP de Microsoft">hilo directo</acronym> dentro de la compañía:
<blockquote>Mary Jo Foley, editor of <a href="http://www.microsoft-watch.com/">Microsoft Watch</a>, said she had no knowledge of the iPod's popularity on Microsoft's campus, but has noticed a lot of iPod chatter among Microsoft's legions of bloggers.

"I have seen lots of Softies blog about it," she wrote in an e-mail.</blockquote>
Las declaraciones de Foley me recuerda a lo que decía en <a href="http://www.bitacoras.org/bit.php?id=95_0_1_0_C">octubre</a>: "Si el producto es bueno, la blogosfera lo hace mejor".
