<p>Vía <a href="http://weblogs.mozillazine.org/ben/archives/007366.html">Inside Firefox</a> e <a href="http://iblnews.com/noticias/01/123363.html">IBLNEWS</a>.</p>

<p>Los rumores sobre los planes de <a href="http://google.com">Google</a> de hacer un navegador basado en <a href="http://www.mozilla.org/products/firefox/">Firefox</a> son recurrentes. Esta noticia contriubye a la especulación: han contratado a <a href="http://weblogs.mozillazine.org/ben/">Ben Goodger</a>, antiguo ingeniero jefe de Firefox.</p>

<p>Goodger, <a href="http://www.bengoodger.com/about/ben.shtml">nacido</a> en Londres hace 24 años, compaginará por igual sus nuevas tareas con la labor que venía realizando para la <a href="http://mozilla.org">Fundación Mozilla</a>. </p>

<p>Lo cuenta en su blog:</p>
<blockquote><a href="http://weblogs.mozillazine.org/ben/archives/007366.html">Changes</a> (24/01/2005)
<br /><br />
(...) As of January 10, 2005, my source of income changed from The Mozilla Foundation to Google, Inc. of Mountain View, California. My role with Firefox and the Mozilla project will remain largely unchanged, I will continue doing much the same work as I have described above - with the new goal of successful 1.1, 1.5 and 2.0 releases. I remain devoted full-time to the advancement of Firefox, the Mozilla platform and web browsing in general. I'm sure you have many questions. While I will be spending more time at Google, I will work out of the Mozilla Foundation offices regularly as the need arises. For all questions regarding Google, I ask that you contact Google directly, rather than myself.</blockquote>
